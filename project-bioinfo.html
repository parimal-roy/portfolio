Bioinformatics Approach to Drug Target Identification

🎯 Project Overview

Application of computational tools for identifying drug targets and molecular docking of anticancer phytochemicals — conducted at a specialized workshop at JUST, Jashore.

🖥️ Computational Approach

STEP 01

Target Identification

Used bioinformatics databases (UniProt, PDB) to identify cancer-relevant protein targets.

STEP 02

Ligand Preparation

Retrieved phytochemical structures from PubChem and prepared 3D conformations.

STEP 03

Molecular Docking

Performed docking using AutoDock Vina to predict binding affinities and interactions.

STEP 04

Visualization & Analysis

Analyzed binding poses and interactions using PyMOL and Discovery Studio.

🧬 Key Learnings

  • Understanding of protein-ligand interaction at molecular level
  • Familiarity with major bioinformatics databases: PDB, UniProt, PubChem
  • Hands-on experience with AutoDock Vina for docking simulations
  • Visualization of 3D molecular structures using PyMOL
  • Introduction to ADMET prediction tools for drug-likeness evaluation

🎯 Significance

This workshop provided foundational knowledge in computational drug discovery — an increasingly critical tool in modern pharmaceutical research. The skills gained bridge traditional wet-lab pharmacology with cutting-edge in silico methodologies, strengthening my research profile for postgraduate applications.
